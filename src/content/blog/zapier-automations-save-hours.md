## Why Zapier?

Not every automation problem needs code. Zapier connects thousands of apps with a drag-and-drop interface, making it accessible to anyone on the team. Here are five automations I rely on every week.

## 1. New GitHub Issues → Slack Notification

When a new issue is opened in any of my repos, Zapier posts a message to my `#dev-alerts` Slack channel. I no longer have to check GitHub constantly — alerts come to me.

## 2. Google Form Submissions → Airtable + Email

Client inquiry forms on my site feed directly into an Airtable base and trigger a personalized confirmation email. The whole flow runs without me touching anything.

## 3. Calendar Events → Time Blocking in Notion

When a meeting is added to my Google Calendar, Zapier creates a matching time-block in my Notion daily planner. My schedule stays in sync automatically.

## 4. RSS Feed → Social Media Draft

Interesting articles I subscribe to get saved as drafts in Buffer for review. This keeps my social presence active without mindless scrolling.

## 5. Stripe Payment → Invoice in Google Drive

Every Stripe payment automatically generates an invoice document in a client folder in Google Drive. Month-end accounting is now a ten-minute job instead of a two-hour one.

## Getting Started

If you're new to Zapier, start with one automation that solves a real pain point. The ROI on learning the tool pays off within the first week. Start with a two-step Zap (trigger + action), get comfortable with the interface, then layer in filters and multi-step flows.
